I · WHAT HUBSPOT ACTUALLY COSTS

Cheap to enter, expensive to need

HubSpot’s pricing is a staircase with a famous step in the middle. Starter costs about €15 per seat per month as of early 2026, and it is genuinely good value, which is the point: your data, your workflows and your team’s habits settle in. Then you need one Professional feature, and the price per seat roughly sextuples, with a mandatory onboarding fee on top of it. The numbers below are the conservative published tiers.

HubSpot Starter · ≈ €15 per seat per month · as of 2026

I.5 seats≈ €900 a year
II.10 seats≈ €1,800 a year
III.25 seats≈ €4,500 a year
IV.Professional, 5 seats≈ €5,400 a year, plus onboarding

The step to Professional is where the lock-in earns its keep

By the time Professional is on the table, leaving feels unthinkable: the CRM holds your entire commercial memory. That is not an accident of pricing. It is the business model, and it is exactly the kind of dependency this company exists to end.

II · THE OWNED EQUIVALENT

Twenty CRM, on your own server

Twenty is a modern open-source CRM: contacts, companies, deal pipelines, notes, tasks, and a full API, backed by a Postgres database that sits on a server you own. It looks and feels like the current generation of SaaS CRMs rather than a nostalgia project, and it is not a hypothetical recommendation: it is the CRM Ironstack itself runs on, receiving the leads from this very website.

The honest framing: HubSpot is not just a CRM, it is a suite. The owned equivalent is a CRM plus an automation layer, wired together. That wiring is real work, which is why this is a service and not a download link.

WHAT MATCHES
  • Contacts, companies, pipelines, notes and tasks, in a clean modern interface.
  • Website forms flowing straight into the CRM, via your own automation.
  • Lead routing, follow-up reminders and enrichment, built with n8n to your rules.
  • A full API, without a paywall deciding which endpoints you deserve.
WHAT DOESN’T
  • HubSpot’s all-in-one marketing suite: landing pages, ads management and campaign email in one roof. The owned version assembles these from separate tools, or honestly skips them.
  • Bulk marketing email needs a proper sending setup; I will not quietly self-host your newsletter into spam folders.
  • The ecosystem of one-click HubSpot integrations. With Twenty, integrations are built, not installed.
WHAT’S BETTER
  • Seats are free. The intern can have an account; so can the bookkeeper.
  • Your commercial history sits in your Postgres, exportable any minute, forever.
  • No feature you rely on can be moved up a tier next quarter.
  • Automations run on your server and can touch all your systems, not just the CRM.

III · WHAT THE MOVE INVOLVES

HubSpot keeps running until we switch

The move follows the five-phase method. For a CRM alone it usually sits at the short end of the four-to-eight-week range, because the data model is small: contacts, companies, deals, notes and their history export cleanly and import into Twenty, where every record is verified against the source.

What does not transfer one-to-one is configuration. Workflows, templates and automations are rebuilt deliberately in n8n rather than copied, which is also the healthiest moment your CRM will ever have: the automations nobody remembers making simply do not get rebuilt.

HubSpot stays live and untouched until validation is done, your team has been walked through the new system, and you approve the switch. The rollback is unusually gentle here: until the day you cancel the subscription, the old system is still there, read-only in practice, holding nothing you have not already taken.

A CRM-only migration like this one starts from €1,750, excl. btw, fixed after the audit.

IV · WHERE YOUR DATA ENDS UP

Your pipeline, inside your walls

After the move, every contact, deal and conversation note lives in a Postgres database on a server you own, in an EU data center, under EU jurisdiction, under your accounts. Your prospects’ personal data stops flowing through an American marketing platform, which means one large entry disappears from your GDPR processor register and no CLOUD Act ambiguity hangs over your sales data.

And because the database is yours, the exit clause is not a promise, it is physics: leaving Ironstack later is a copy operation, not a data ransom negotiation.

Fair questions

Is there a real self-hosted alternative to HubSpot for a small company?

For the CRM core, yes: Twenty covers contacts, companies, pipelines, notes, tasks and a full API, and it is what runs Ironstack’s own sales. For the full marketing suite around it, the honest answer is that no single open-source tool replaces everything; the automation and email pieces come from separate tools, wired together.

Do we lose our HubSpot data if we leave?

No. Contacts, companies and deals export cleanly and import into Twenty, where they land in a Postgres database on your own server. What does not transfer one-to-one is configuration: workflows and email templates are rebuilt deliberately, which is also the moment to drop the ones nobody remembers making.

Can a self-hosted CRM send our marketing emails?

Campaign email is a separate concern from the CRM, and bulk sending from your own server is a deliverability trap. The honest setup is a self-hosted campaign tool or the CRM feeding an EU email service with its own sending reputation. I will not quietly self-host your newsletter and let it land in spam.

What does HubSpot actually cost compared to self-hosting?

HubSpot Starter runs about €15 per seat per month, and the Professional tiers jump to roughly €90 per seat plus a mandatory onboarding fee, as of early 2026. The owned equivalent runs on server capacity that costs a few euros a month regardless of seats; what you pay for is the build, once: a migration from €1,750, excl. btw, fixed after the audit.

How long does a HubSpot migration take?

For a CRM alone, usually at the short end of the four-to-eight-week range, because the data model is small: contacts, companies, deals, notes. HubSpot keeps running until the new system is validated and your team has been walked through it.

Does Twenty CRM integrate with our website forms?

Yes, over its API. This exact website is the demonstration: the contact form flows through self-hosted automation into Twenty without a single third-party processor in between. The stack register shows the whole chain.
