“If your business runs on someone else’s cloud, your data is part of someone else’s business model. It doesn’t have to be.”

Every migration Ironstack does starts from one conviction: a company should be able to point at its infrastructure and say ours. It should not be rented, mined, or one price increase away from a problem. It should be owned, understood, and documented.

The method

Five controlled phases. This is how an existing environment moves onto infrastructure you own. Your current environment keeps running untouched until we deliberately switch. The switch happens in a window you approve, with a tested rollback plan.

  1. I

    Discovery & Audit

    I map your infrastructure, dependencies, costs, and risks. You receive a written audit that stays useful even if we never work together again.

  2. II

    Architecture & Planning

    Target design plus a step-by-step migration runbook with a rollback per step. Nothing executes without your sign-off.

  3. III

    Migration Execution

    The new environment is built and filled while your production keeps running, untouched.

  4. IV

    Validation & Cutover

    Full testing, then the switch in your approved window, followed by close monitoring.

  5. V

    Handover & Custodianship

    Documentation, knowledge transfer, and a trained team. The old environment is safely decommissioned, and Managed Sovereignty takes over from there.

Fair questions

You are one person. What happens when you are ill or on holiday?

Monitoring never sleeps, and everything I build is documented so no knowledge exists only in my head. Planned absences are announced well in advance, and retainer clients get honestly defined response windows. I will not pretend to be an agency: you get one person, and that person answers.

How long does a migration take?

It depends on the size of your environment. A typical small-business migration runs four to eight weeks from audit to handover. The audit gives you an exact timeline before you commit to anything.

What does it cost?

The audit has a small fixed price. AI and automation work has a fixed price per project. The Stack has a fixed setup fee. Managed Sovereignty has a fixed monthly price per tier. Migrations have a fixed project fee based on the audit findings. You always know the number before anything starts, and the number does not move.

Do we have to migrate everything at once?

No. Migrations are phased by design, and the audit usually identifies quick wins. Starting with a single system is a perfectly good way to begin.

Is self-hosted actually safe enough for a business?

Safety comes from practice, not from a vendor logo. Updates, backups, monitoring, and hardening are part of every environment I hand over. What you gain on top is certainty: you know exactly where your data is and who can touch it.

What if we want to leave Ironstack later?

Then you leave, and you take everything with you. That is the exit clause above, and it is written into the agreement. Lock-in would contradict everything this company stands for.

Where does our data live, and what about the GDPR?

On servers you own, in an EU data center, under EU jurisdiction. With no American cloud provider in the chain there is no CLOUD Act ambiguity, your GDPR processor register gets shorter instead of longer, and “where is our data?” becomes a question you can answer by pointing.

And AI? Can we use it without leaking data?

Yes, if you are deliberate about it. The biggest AI risk in most companies today is employees pasting client data into free chatbots. I build the deliberate version: assistants and automations that work against your own systems, on your own server where possible, and through a disclosed EU-hosted model where not. The audit can map your current AI exposure as well.
