WHY THERE IS NO PRICE LIST

The number comes before the work

There are no prices on this website, and that is not a sales trick. Every environment is genuinely different: five people on Google Workspace with one shared drive is a different job than twenty-five on Microsoft 365 with ten years of SharePoint history. A price list would either be padded to cover the unknown or wrong the moment it met your environment. I refuse both.

So the number comes from looking first. A free 30-minute call to find out whether this fits at all. Then the audit, which maps what is actually there. Then a proposal with the numbers in writing, before any build starts: what the work costs, and what the monthly care costs.

What you can hold me to: the numbers in the proposal are fixed and do not move, and the exit clause applies every single month. If a number ever stopped being fair, you could simply leave. That is the point of writing it down.

The method

Every build runs through the same five controlled phases, whether we install a fresh stack or move an existing environment. Your current setup keeps running untouched until we deliberately switch, in a window you approve, with a tested rollback plan. Smaller automation projects follow the same discipline, just lighter.

  1. I

    Discovery & Audit

    I map your infrastructure, dependencies, costs, and risks. You receive a written audit that stays useful even if we never work together again.

  2. II

    Architecture & Planning

    Target design plus a step-by-step migration runbook with a rollback per step. Nothing executes without your sign-off.

  3. III

    The Build

    The new environment is built and filled next to your production, which keeps running untouched.

  4. IV

    Validation & Go-live

    Full testing, then the switch or launch in a window you approve, followed by close monitoring.

  5. V

    Handover & Custodianship

    Documentation, knowledge transfer, and a trained team. The old environment is safely decommissioned, and Managed Sovereignty takes over from there.

AI, on your terms

Every business is being told to adopt AI, and almost every AI product wants your data in someone else’s cloud. That is the same trade this company exists to end. So AI at Ironstack follows one rule: the model comes to your data, your data does not go to the model.

◆

An assistant where you already talk

A private assistant in Telegram, WhatsApp or your team chat, and on the phone if you want it to have a voice. It searches your files, books appointments, creates tasks and drafts invoices, all against your own systems.

◆

Automations that think

The automation layer that wires your stack together can read now: leads get triaged, documents filed, meeting notes turned into tasks. AI is used where it earns its place, and skipped where a plain rule does the job better.

◆

Answers from your own knowledge

A private search across your wiki, documents and archives that answers in plain language, with sources. Indexed on your server, inside your walls, without becoming someone else’s training data.

The honest version: small open models run on your own hardware, and some tasks need bigger ones. When they do, I use an EU-hosted model under a written agreement, disclosed in your documentation, and only for the data that task needs. You always know which is which.

Fair questions

You are one person. What happens when you are ill or on holiday?

Monitoring never sleeps, and everything I build is documented so no knowledge exists only in my head. Planned absences are announced well in advance, and retainer clients get honestly defined response windows. I will not pretend to be an agency: you get one person, and that person answers.

How long does a migration take?

It depends on the size of your environment. A typical small-business migration runs four to eight weeks from audit to handover. The audit gives you an exact timeline before you commit to anything.

What does it cost?

The audit has a small fixed price. AI and automation work has a fixed price per project. The Stack has a fixed setup fee. Managed Sovereignty has a fixed monthly price per tier. Migrations have a fixed project fee based on the audit findings. You always know the number before anything starts, and the number does not move.

Do we have to migrate everything at once?

No. Migrations are phased by design, and the audit usually identifies quick wins. Starting with a single system is a perfectly good way to begin.

Is self-hosted actually safe enough for a business?

Safety comes from practice, not from a vendor logo. Updates, backups, monitoring, and hardening are part of every environment I hand over. What you gain on top is certainty: you know exactly where your data is and who can touch it.

What if we want to leave Ironstack later?

Then you leave, and you take everything with you. That is the exit clause above, and it is written into the agreement. Lock-in would contradict everything this company stands for.

Where does our data live, and what about the GDPR?

On servers you own, in an EU data center, under EU jurisdiction. With no American cloud provider in the chain there is no CLOUD Act ambiguity, your GDPR processor register gets shorter instead of longer, and “where is our data?” becomes a question you can answer by pointing.

And AI? Can we use it without leaking data?

Yes, if you are deliberate about it. The biggest AI risk in most companies today is employees pasting client data into free chatbots. I build the deliberate version: assistants and automations that work against your own systems, on your own server where possible, and through a disclosed EU-hosted model where not. The audit can map your current AI exposure as well.
